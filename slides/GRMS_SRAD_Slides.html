Gravel Road Management System (GRMS)

System Requirements Analysis & Design (SRAD)

Project: GRMS System Requirements Analysis & Design
Date: November 2025

The Problem

  • RRAs rely on fragmented tools such as spreadsheets, paper forms, and isolated databases.
  • Inconsistent data creates duplication of effort and slows decision-making.
  • Maintenance work is typically reactive rather than planned.
  • Funding requests often lack reliable, data-driven justification.

Project Goal & Objectives

Overall Goal

Modernize RRA gravel road management with an integrated, data-driven system that centralizes data and improves transparency.

Key Objectives

  • Create a single source of truth for road and structure inventories.
  • Standardize condition surveys, traffic counts, and planning workflows.
  • Enable decision support with prioritization and costing tools.
  • Use automated analysis (e.g., MCI) to accelerate maintenance planning.

Success Criteria (Phase 1)

  • Deliver a working prototype on schedule.
  • Generate a prioritized maintenance list in under 10 minutes.
  • Successfully train at least three RRA users.
  • Achieve ≥98% accuracy for imported data.
  • Receive positive feedback from RRA management.

Key Stakeholders

  • RRA Management (Directors/Planners) for reports and funding justification.
  • RRA Engineers and Technicians who collect data and plan maintenance.
  • GIS/IT Specialists who maintain databases, GIS layers, and availability.
  • Donor Agencies & Ministry stakeholders needing condition and fund-use reports.
  • Development Team responsible for building GRMS and training staff.

Scope: In-Scope (Phase 1) – Part 1

  • Road Inventory: registry of roads, sections, segments, and key attributes.
  • Condition Survey Module: record defects using ERA standards and compute MCI.
  • Structure Inventory & Inspections: catalog bridges and culverts with condition records.

Scope: In-Scope (Phase 1) – Part 2

  • Traffic Data Management with ADT/PCU calculations and seasonal adjustments.
  • Decision Support catalog of maintenance actions, unit costs, and recommendations.
  • Prioritization Engine using multi-criteria ranking.
  • Reporting & Export to Excel, PDF, and GIS formats.

Scope: Out-of-Scope (Future Phases)

  • Phase 2 offline mobile survey app with sync capabilities.
  • Future rich web UI (modern SPA) replacing basic Django admin pages.
  • Advanced GIS mapping with interactive layers and spatial analysis.
  • Enhanced integrations, aerial imagery, and AI-driven defect detection.

System Architecture Overview

  • Backend built on Django (Python).
  • PostgreSQL + PostGIS database for spatial data.
  • Browser-based UI via Django templates/Admin in Phase 1.
  • RESTful JSON APIs for integrations and future mobile apps.
  • Secure API endpoints for ERA data exchange.
  • Modular presentation, application, and data layers for scalability.
  • Cloud-agnostic deployment that supports local or cloud servers.

High-Level Data Flow

  1. Field engineers collect condition and traffic data.
  2. Data is entered or synchronized into the GRMS database.
  3. The system computes MCI and traffic metrics, then identifies interventions.
  4. Planners run the prioritization engine and apply budget constraints.
  5. Reports, dashboards, and maps are produced for stakeholders.
  6. Key data synchronizes with national databases via secure APIs.

Technology Stack

  • Backend: Django (Python).
  • Database: PostgreSQL + PostGIS.
  • Web Front-End (Phase 1): Django templates/Admin (HTML/JS).
  • Mobile (Phase 2): Android app or progressive web app.
  • GIS Tools: exports compatible with ArcGIS/QGIS via Shapefile or GeoJSON.
  • Standards: RESTful APIs, SQL, GeoJSON, and open-source tooling.

Core Module: Condition & Structures

  • Record detailed defects with severity and extent per segment.
  • Automatically calculate Maintenance Condition Index (0–100).
  • Classify segments as Good, Fair, Poor, or Bad and link recommended actions.
  • Maintain a registry of structures with IDs, locations, and materials.
  • Track inspection dates, component ratings, and integrate with road plans.

Core Module: Prioritization Engine

  • Ranks road segments and structures to identify critical repairs.
  • Uses weighted scoring for condition, traffic, population, strategic importance, and safety.
  • Outputs priority scores and ranked lists for evidence-based maintenance plans.

Non-Functional Requirements

  • Prioritization runs for thousands of segments in under 10 minutes.
  • Dashboards load in roughly three seconds.
  • >99% uptime during business hours with automated backups.
  • Security: HTTPS-only, RBAC, input validation, and audit logging.
  • Localization-ready with support for multiple languages.

Implementation Roadmap

  • Phase 1 (6–9 months): build core modules, pilot, and deliver prototype work plan.
  • Phase 2 (3–6 months): pilot deployment, offline mobile app, richer GIS maps, and integration testing.
  • Phase 3 (6+ months): national scale-up, full mobile/AI rollout, and transition to ERA IT.

Transformative Impact

  • Shift from reactive, fragmented planning to data-driven prioritization.
  • Enable effective resource allocation and defensible funding requests.
  • Provide a modular, future-proof system ready for mobile and AI enhancements.
